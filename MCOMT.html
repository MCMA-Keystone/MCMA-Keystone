About

The MCOMT is one of the largest and most influential events hosted by the MCMA. It is held twice annually in spring and fall. The current director/supervisor of MCMA's MCOMT committee is Kevin Shi.

General Information

The MCOMT is a 40-minute exam. It consists of 8 fill-in-the-blanks questions, with each question being worth 5 points. The maximum achievable score on the MCOMT is 40 points. High-scorers (to be determined by the MCOMT committee) are selected to attend the invitational round (MCIME).

Difficulty

Compared to the MCIME, the MCOMT isn't a very difficult competition. The difficulty of the questions on the MCOMT roughly range from 2-3.5 out of 10 (where 10 is the difficulty of the International Mathematical Olympiad). However, it covers a broad spectrum of high-school precalculus topics, and requires a certain amount of critical thinking as well as high proficiency in computation and high-school concepts.

Prerequisites

There are no prerequisites for the MCOMT. However, it is generally recommended that the contestant is a secondary school student with an interest in mathematics.
